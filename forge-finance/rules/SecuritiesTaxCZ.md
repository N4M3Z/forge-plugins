Czech tax treatment of securities sales and investment income for individuals ([zákon 586/1992 Sb.][2]).

## Classification

| Income type         | § section | DPFO location | Tax base                          |
| ------------------- | --------- | ------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Stock/ETF sales     | §10       | Příloha 2     | proceeds minus cost minus fees    |
| Dividends           | §8        | Řádek 38      | gross amount (no cost deduction)  |
| Interest (foreign)  | §8        | Řádek 38      | gross amount                      |

§10 losses offset §10 gains in the same year but cannot carry forward or offset other categories ([§10(4)][3]).

## Time-Test Exemption (§4(1)(w))

Securities held over **3 years** are exempt from tax. Each lot is tested independently (FIFO). Fractional shares retain the exemption.

From 2025: a **40 million CZK annual cap** on time-test-exempt income was introduced ([zákon 349/2023 Sb.][4]). Exempt proceeds above this cap become taxable.

**2025 transitional step-up**: for securities held before 1 January 2025, taxpayers may elect the market value as of 31 December 2024 as the cost basis for future sales ([Grant Thornton CZ][7]).

## 100,000 CZK Gross Threshold

If total **gross proceeds** (not profit) from all securities sales in the year do not exceed 100,000 CZK, the income is fully exempt and need not be declared ([§4(1)(v)][5]).

## Foreign Currency Conversion

Two methods for individuals not keeping `účetnictví`:

| Method                   | Source                              | When to use                    |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| `jednotný kurz` (annual) | GFŘ, published post-year-end        | simpler, one rate per year     |
| `denní kurz ČNB` (daily) | [ČNB exchange rates][1]             | more precise, per-transaction  |

**Must use the same method for both purchase and sale legs.** Cannot mix methods within a filing.

Reference: [kurzy.cz/jednotny-kurz][6] for annual rates, [ČNB daily rates][1] for per-transaction rates.

## US Dividend Withholding (W-8BEN)

With W-8BEN: 15% US withholding at source (treaty rate). Without: 30%.

Czech treatment: declare gross dividend (pre-withholding) under §8. Credit the 15% US tax via **Příloha č. 3** (foreign tax credit). In practice, 15% US + 0% CZ = 15% total, matching the Czech 15% rate.
