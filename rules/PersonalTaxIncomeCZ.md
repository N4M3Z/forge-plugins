Czech personal income tax (DPFO) income categories under [zákon 586/1992 Sb.][1]

| §   | Czech                                | English              | Scope                                                |
| --- | ------------------------------------ | -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| §6  | `příjmy ze závislé činnosti`         | employment income    | wages, salaries, bonuses, benefits                   |
| §7  | `příjmy ze samostatné činnosti`      | self-employment      | business, freelance, agriculture, IP                 |
| §8  | `příjmy z kapitálového majetku`      | capital income       | dividends, interest (mostly withheld at source, §36) |
| §9  | `příjmy z nájmu`                     | rental income        | real estate and movable property outside business    |
| §10 | `ostatní příjmy`                     | other income         | occasional sales, crypto, prizes, unclassified       |

Tax rate: 15% up to 48x average salary, 23% above (two-bracket system from 2024).

§7 flat-rate expenses (`paušální výdaje`): 80% agriculture/crafts, 60% business, 40% IP (cap 800 000 CZK), 30% rental.

§10 exempt if total under 30 000 CZK/year. Losses carry forward for §7 and §9 only (up to 5 years). §6 losses cannot offset other categories.

Filing required when: multiple concurrent employers, self-employment over 6 000 CZK, other taxable income over 6 000 CZK, or untaxed foreign income. Single-employer employees with `prohlášení poplatníka` use `roční zúčtování` instead.

Foreign income uses the applicable [double taxation treaty][2]: `zápočet daně` (credit method) or `vynětí příjmů` (exemption method). Note: [§38f ZDP][3] can override the treaty method for employment income — e.g., Czech-Swiss treaty specifies credit, but §38f applies `vynětí s výhradou progrese` (exemption with progression) for qualifying employment income. Always check both the treaty and the domestic implementing provision.
