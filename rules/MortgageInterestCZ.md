Mortgage interest deduction (`úroky z úvěru na bytové potřeby`) under [§15 odst. 3, 4][1]. Analogous to US mortgage interest deduction, but reduces the tax base directly.

Eligible loans: `hypoteční úvěr` or `úvěr ze stavebního spoření` used for `bytové potřeby` (own permanent residence — purchase, construction, renovation). Investment properties do not qualify.

| Contract signed              | Annual limit |
| ---------------------------- | ------------ |
| Before January 1, 2021       | 300 000 CZK |
| On or after January 1, 2021  | 150 000 CZK |

Limit is per household, not per person. Co-borrowers share the limit. Only one spouse can claim per year. Refinancing preserves the deduction if the new loan exclusively repays the original housing debt; the limit follows the original contract date. Cash-out refinancing: only the portion matching the original principal qualifies.

Required documents each year:

| Document                            | Purpose                             | Needed       |
| ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------ |
| `Potvrzení o zaplacených úrocích`   | annual interest paid                | every year   |
| `Smlouva o úvěru`                   | proves loan is for `bytové potřeby` | first year   |
| `List vlastnictví`                  | confirms property ownership         | if requested |

When validating: confirm `bytové potřeby` (own residence, not investment), check contract date for limit, verify claimed amount matches `potvrzení`, confirm taxpayer is party to the loan, check spousal claim exclusivity, verify contract is in project Assets.
